What is Your Financial Independence Number?
Personal finance and investing towards financial independence is a wonderful thing but let's be clear, it is not the goal of life. The ultimate goal of life is how you and I can live more, grow more and contribute more. It just happens that money is a central part the life connection.
Whereas money is not the most important thing in life, Zig Ziglar points out that it is reasonably close to oxygen in the "Gotta Have It"Â scale in today's economic-centred world. We certainly need money for needs but we also need it for our wants and desires - be they for personal fulfillment, helping others or taking advantage of opportunities. We need money to do good to the world - be it in philanthropy or spreading a message. Those who know me know that I don't have much need or regard for money to spend on myself - I work hard to make money but I give it all to causes I believe in. Unlike the great philanthropists who spend a lifetime amassing it then start giving away in later years or after they die, I much prefer the philosophy of the great Brazilian philanthropist Ricardo Semler - "Give it away as you make it." Even when you have altruistic intentions with money, you must consider this question:
What is your Financial Independence Number?
In other words, how much money must you receive from your investments (e.g. rental income) so that you no longer have to trade your hours for shillings in order to survive financially. Working a job or running a business is not a bad thing. Serving others with your time, energy and skills can be incredibly fulfilling. Even a great job can become a pain in the neck when you know you must do it for the money. Instead of happiness and growth, a less than ideal job trapsÂ you in the rat race. You draw a line in the sand beyond which you will no longer financially need a job. Even before you get there, destination gives you hope and encouragement and the journey gives your life meaning. Its was absurd, it was unreasonable. We didn't know how to do it, but we knew a few things:
  1. We were determined both to work hard to increase our employment income.

Reaching your Financial Independence Number does not mean that you can't still work and make more money. It does not mean that you will stop growing financially for the rest of your life. It does not mean that you are perfectly safe forever. Achieving this goal only means that the pressure is off. Your investments could support you instead of your job. This is an incredible place to be. As you get closer to this goal, an entire universe of possibilities opens up. The same job you hated could be transformed when you know you are there by choice. The business idea you fearedÂ starting now could be a feasible idea. Creativity and energy that had been dormant for years will suddenly re-emerge. New opportunities, new businesses, new questions and new exciting paths will open themselves to you. So, whatever a better life means to you, however you define 'Needs, Wants and Desires', I recommend you figure out your own Financial Independence Number. It will provide a clear, focussed and compelling vision to work towards.
